Proposed Research: Lightweight Ephemeral Agents for Predictive Threat Detection

As critical systems increasingly rely on interconnected IoT devices, traditional reactive cybersecurity mechanisms are becoming insufficient. Resource-constrained environments—such as implantable medical devices (IMDs) and industrial controllers—require security solutions that are both energy-efficient and proactive. This research proposes the design and evaluation of ephemeral, event-triggered security agents that integrate lightweight anomaly detection techniques to identify and mitigate threats before system failure occurs.

Research Objective

The primary goal of this study is to answer the following question:

"Can lightweight, event-triggered anomaly detection agents provide effective early threat detection in resource-constrained IoT devices while maintaining minimal energy and memory overhead?"

Expected Contribution & Evaluation

This study aims to contribute a novel architecture for transient, low-overhead security agents alongside a quantitative evaluation of event-triggered anomaly detection in constrained environments. By shifting from continuous monitoring to event-driven mechanisms, the research explores a pathway toward predictive cybersecurity. Performance will be evaluated based on detection accuracy, false-positive rates, and memory overhead compared to persistent monitoring systems.
